PULL-UPS

  • Exercise Overview:

    • Pull-ups are a compound upper-body exercise that primarily targets the muscles of the back, including the latissimus dorsi, rhomboids, and trapezius, as well as the biceps and forearms.

  • Execution:
    1. Start by gripping a pull-up bar with your hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart, palms facing away from you.
    2. Hang with your arms fully extended and your core engaged.
    3. Pull yourself up by driving your elbows down and back, aiming to bring your chin above the bar.
    4. Lower yourself back down to the starting position with control, fully extending your arms.

  • Tips for Proper Form:
    • Maintain a straight body line throughout the movement, avoiding swinging or kipping.
    • Engage your core muscles to stabilize your body and prevent excessive swinging.
    • Focus on pulling with your back muscles rather than relying solely on your arms.
    • Start with assisted pull-ups or negatives if you're unable to perform full pull-ups initially.
    • Gradually increase the number of repetitions and sets as you build strength.

SQUAT

  • Exercise overview

    • Squats are a fundamental lower-body exercise that primarily targets the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, and core muscles.

  • Execution:
    1. Begin by standing with your feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly turned out.
    2. Engage your core and maintain a neutral spine as you initiate the movement by bending your hips and knees.
    3. Lower your body down by pushing your hips back and bending your knees, keeping your chest up and your weight in your heels.
    4. Descend until your thighs are parallel to the ground or slightly below, ensuring your knees track in line with your toes.
    5. Drive through your heels to return to the starting position, fully extending your hips and knees

  • Tips for Proper Form:
    • Keep your chest up and your back flat throughout the movement to maintain a neutral spine.
    • Avoid allowing your knees to collapse inward; instead, push them out in line with your toes.
    • Engage your glutes and core muscles to stabilize your pelvis and maintain balance.
    • Start with bodyweight squats or use a support (like a chair or wall) if you're new to the exercise.
    • Gradually increase the depth and resistance as you become more proficient and build strength.
